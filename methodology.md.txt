Operational AI Governance Methodology

Introduction

This framework follows a structured methodology for designing operational governance architectures for Artificial Intelligence systems.

Rather than beginning with implementation, the process starts by understanding the organisation, its objectives, its operational environment and the level of governance required.

The resulting architecture is designed specifically for the organisation being analysed.

---

Step 1 — Understand the Organisation

The first stage consists of analysing the organisation itself.

Typical considerations include:

* Business objectives
* Operational processes
* Organisational structure
* Decision workflows
* Existing governance policies
* Regulatory environment

At this stage, no governance architecture is produced.

The objective is to understand the operating environment.

---

Step 2 — Identify Governance Requirements

After understanding the organisation, governance requirements are identified.

Examples include:

* Human oversight requirements
* Decision boundaries
* Behavioural expectations
* Risk tolerance
* Transparency requirements
* Escalation policies

---

Step 3 — Context and Risk Analysis

Every organisation operates under different levels of operational risk.

The methodology evaluates contextual factors such as:

* ambiguity
* operational impact
* decision pressure
* organisational sensitivity

These factors influence how AI should behave in different situations.

---

Step 4 — Design the Governance Architecture

Once requirements have been identified, an operational governance architecture is designed.

This architecture defines:

* behavioural layers
* operational rules
* decision behaviour
* context adaptation
* oversight mechanisms

The architecture is unique to each organisation.

There is no universal governance model.

---

Step 5 — Operational Representation

Only after the architecture has been designed are governance rules represented in structured operational formats.

These representations are implementation artefacts rather than the architecture itself.

The architecture always precedes implementation.

---

Step 6 — Validation

The resulting governance model is reviewed to ensure:

* internal consistency
* behavioural predictability
* proportionality
* alignment with organisational objectives
* preservation of human decision authority

---

Methodological Principle

The central principle of this methodology is that governance should never begin with implementation.

Operational structures are the consequence of architectural design, not its starting point.
