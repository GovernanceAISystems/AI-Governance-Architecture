# Architecture

Operational AI Governance Architecture

Introduction

The Operational AI Governance Framework proposes an architectural approach for governing AI systems through structured behavioural layers.

Instead of relying exclusively on prompts or static instructions, the framework separates governance into multiple operational components, each responsible for a specific aspect of AI behaviour.

The objective is to improve consistency, transparency, proportionality and human oversight while allowing the AI system to remain adaptable to different organisational environments.

---

Architectural Principles

The framework is based on five fundamental principles:

* Behaviour should be governed before responses are generated.
* Governance must adapt to context.
* Human authority always takes precedence over AI decisions.
* Behaviour should remain consistent across similar situations.
* Operational governance should be represented in structured, executable models.

---

        ▼

---

Architectural Layers

1. Cognitive Core

Defines the permanent behavioural principles of the system.

This layer establishes how the AI reasons before producing any response.

---

2. Behavioural Governance

Defines behavioural policies including:

* response priorities
* language proportionality
* authority limits
* behavioural consistency
* non-action conditions

---

3. Context Evaluation

Continuously evaluates:

* ambiguity
* perceived risk
* decision pressure
* operational context

Behaviour is dynamically adjusted according to these factors.

---

4. Decision Behaviour

Determines how the AI should behave under different situations.

Examples include:

* increasing caution
* requesting clarification
* reducing certainty
* returning decisions to human operators

---

5. Output Mediation

Applies governance controls immediately before response generation.

Its purpose is to ensure that generated outputs remain consistent with organisational governance policies.

---

6. Human Oversight

The framework assumes that AI assists decision-making but does not replace human authority.

Governance mechanisms ensure that strategic decisions remain under human responsibility.

---

Design Goal

The architecture is intended to demonstrate how governance can become an operational layer within AI systems rather than existing solely as documentation or policy.
