Operational AI Governance — Example Use Cases

Introduction

The Operational AI Governance Framework is designed to be adaptable across different organisational environments.

The following examples are illustrative scenarios showing how a governance architecture may vary according to business context, operational risk and decision requirements.

---

Financial Services

Typical Governance Requirements

* High regulatory exposure
* Strong audit requirements
* Human approval for critical decisions
* Risk-based behaviour
* Consistent customer interactions

Possible governance architecture:

* Increased behavioural caution
* Mandatory human oversight
* Decision escalation
* Complete interaction traceability

---

Healthcare

Typical Governance Requirements

* High safety requirements
* Clinical uncertainty
* Human responsibility
* Conservative AI behaviour

Possible governance architecture:

* High caution mode
* Explicit uncertainty handling
* Recommendation limits
* Medical professional remains final decision maker

---

Enterprise Customer Support

Typical Governance Requirements

* Fast operational responses
* Consistent communication
* Escalation of complex situations
* Customer-friendly language

Possible governance architecture:

* Dynamic behavioural adaptation
* Context-sensitive responses
* Escalation policies
* Behavioural consistency

---

Internal Corporate Assistants

Typical Governance Requirements

* Internal knowledge access
* Policy compliance
* Confidentiality
* Organisational consistency

Possible governance architecture:

* Policy-aware behaviour
* Restricted operational boundaries
* Context-sensitive decision support
* Human validation where required

---

Executive Decision Support

Typical Governance Requirements

* Strategic analysis
* Multiple alternatives
* Risk assessment
* No autonomous decision making

Possible governance architecture:

* Structured analytical summaries
* Trade-off presentation
* Decision returned to leadership
* No prescriptive conclusions

---

Key Observation

Although the operational structures may be represented using similar mechanisms, governance architectures should never be identical across organisations.

Every organisation has different objectives, different operational risks and different governance requirements.

The architecture must therefore be designed specifically for the environment in which the AI system will operate.
